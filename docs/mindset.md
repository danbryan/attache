# The Attaché mindset

AI agents changed how work gets done, but not how it gets watched. The agent
works asynchronously; you still sit there synchronously, eyes on a terminal,
waiting to find out if you are needed. The bottleneck stopped being the work
and became your attention.

Attaché exists to break that habit. Three shifts:

**1. Delegate like a manager, not a babysitter.**
You would not stand behind a good employee watching them type. Hand the task
over, walk away, and expect a report when something is done or a decision is
needed. Attaché is that report: spoken, short, in a voice and persona you
chose, filed as a voicemail you can replay whenever you get back.

**2. Interrupts over polling.**
Checking the terminal every few minutes is polling, and polling is the most
expensive thing you do all day because it costs you your focus. Attaché flips
the direction: it stays quiet while agents run, and interrupts you only when
one is genuinely waiting on you, through a real macOS notification that
respects Focus and Do Not Disturb. Trust the interrupt. Ignore everything
else until you feel like listening.

**3. Updates you enjoy are updates you don't ignore.**
Raw agent output is written to be complete, not to be heard. The personality
layer is not a gimmick: when the recap sounds like a calm senior engineer, a
radio dispatcher, or whatever fits your culture, in your language, at your
speed, you actually listen. Alert fatigue is a design problem, and the fix is
making the signal pleasant.

Run one agent and Attaché is a courtesy. Run three or four and it becomes the
only sane way to work: a chief of staff that watches the whole fleet, files
every result, speaks up when someone needs you, and gives you your screen, and
your attention, back.

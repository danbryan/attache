# The Attaché mindset

AI agents changed how the work gets done, but not how it gets watched. The agent
runs on its own schedule; you still sit there in real time, eyes on a terminal,
waiting to find out whether it needs you. The bottleneck stopped being the work
and became your attention.

Attaché is built to break that habit. Three shifts.

**1. Delegate like a manager, not a babysitter.**
You would not stand behind someone good and watch them work. You hand the task
over, you step away, and you expect a report when something is done or a
decision is needed. It does not matter whether the task is code, research, a
draft, or a pile of files to sort. Attaché is that report: spoken, short, in a
voice and personality you chose, waiting in an inbox you can play back whenever
you get to it.

**2. Interrupts over polling.**
Checking on the work every few minutes is polling, and polling is expensive
because the cost is your focus, not your time. Attaché flips the direction. It
stays quiet while agents run and speaks up only when one is actually waiting on
you, through a real macOS notification that respects Focus and Do Not Disturb.
Trust the interrupt. Ignore everything else until you feel like listening.

**3. Updates you enjoy are updates you do not tune out.**
Raw agent output is written to be complete, not to be heard. The personality
layer is not decoration. When the briefing sounds like a calm colleague, or a
dispatcher, or whatever fits the way you think, in your language and at your
pace, you actually take it in. Alert fatigue is a design problem, and the fix is
making the signal pleasant enough to keep.

Run one agent and Attaché is a convenience. Run three or four and it is the only
sane way to work: something that watches the whole set, files every result,
speaks up when one of them needs you, and gives you back your screen and your
attention.

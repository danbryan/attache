Privacy: what "private" means, and extending it beyond live calls

Answering your questions precisely

QuestionAnswer
Encrypted on disk, or incognito?Incognito. Private call means no record is created: no history cards, no direct-chat capsules, no memory proposals, and the in-memory turn state is erased at hangup. It is not an encryption feature. Disk encryption is FileVault's job; Attaché's stores are additionally locked to your user account (0600/0700 permissions, hardened in the audit).
What does it cover: what I say, or what Attaché says?Both directions of the conversation, plus derived artifacts (capsules, proposals). What it does not change is transport: if the active personality uses a cloud model, the turn still goes to that provider under the existing egress rules. Private controls the record, egress class controls the wire. The UI should say this in one line: "Private call with a local model: nothing leaves this Mac. Private call with a cloud model: no record kept here."
Voicemails on a call?Correct, there are none: voicemail cards come from watched agent sessions, not from calls. Which is exactly why privacy needs a second surface:

New concept: Private Session (privacy without a call)

Design The voicemail-side answer to your "what if someone wants a session private but they're not in a live call" question. A per-session control with two actions:

  • Don't record this session: while enabled, the session produces no persisted cards (playback becomes ephemeral, like the recap fallback path), is excluded from the FTS index and session maps, and builds no capsules. Watching and live narration still work; nothing is written.
  • Forget this session: retroactive scrub. Hard-deletes every card, index entry, capsule, and alternate take linked to that session. The machinery exists: conversation-scoped secure delete landed in the audit commit, and the FTS index already supports invalidation.
One-way door, same as calls: a session can become private mid-flight (with retroactive scrub), but a private session's past is never retroactively persisted. And fail closed: if any scrub step fails, the UI says "still recorded" rather than pretending.

The incognito indicator

Your crown-band instinct is right, and it should be layered like Chrome's treatment (which works because you cannot miss it):

  • The character wears it: a visor band in the crown area of the character for the entire private call or private session. The character is the emotional center of the app; making it visibly go incognito is the strongest possible signal, and it is on-brand in a way a mere icon is not. Echo (voice bars) gets a matching treatment on its frame.
  • The surface says it: a persistent PRIVATE chip in the call HUD and on the session row, plus a subtle accent-tint shift on the window edge. Also shown in the mini window.
  • Accessibility announces it: VoiceOver announcement on entering and leaving private mode, and the state is in the AX tree so the smoke harness can assert it.

Right-click menus, with the Option convention

Your sound-menu observation is the real macOS convention: Option is the "power tier" modifier (Option-click the volume icon for input devices, Option in menus swaps Save for Save As, Option-click Wi-Fi for diagnostics). AppKit even supports it natively: an NSMenuItem can be marked as the alternate of another, and the menu swaps live while Option is held. So this costs almost nothing to adopt properly.

Rules, then menus

  • Option reveals power or destructive variants of visible items. It is never the only path to a feature; everything reachable with Option is also reachable somewhere discoverable.
  • Privacy is never hidden behind Option. Start Private Call stays a first-class visible item.
  • Every item shows its keyboard equivalent, carries an AX label, and gets a ui-smoke assertion.

Character yielding: what I meant, and what to verify

You likely already satisfy most of this; the report line was a design guardrail, not a defect claim. Reserved space answers the layout half: the character never occludes captions or cards. The other half is attention: motion in peripheral vision pulls the eye even when nothing overlaps, and reading karaoke captions is exactly the moment the eye should not be pulled. Concretely, the checklist worth a one-time audit:

  • While captions are actively highlighting words, the character's ambient motion should damp to near-still (breathing only), and the rare-idle animation should be suppressed entirely.
  • When a palette or overlay (⌘K, ⌘I, ⌘Y, ⇧⌘P) is open, the character behind it should hold pose rather than animate under the blur.
  • Reduce Motion honors all of the above unconditionally.

If those three already hold, this item closes with no work. If any fails, each is a small, contained fix in the renderer's activity gating.

Roundtable: multiple Attachés discussing a session

Parked by your call, and I agree with parking it — but I want to be clear: I think this is the best product idea you have raised, and here is my honest read.

Why it fits this app unusually wellYou already have the atoms nobody else has: personalities as complete units with distinct voices and temperaments, "another take" proving multi-personality narration over one artifact, frozen-session authorization so every panelist argues from the same verified evidence, and a listening-first user base. For the "I can't articulate my objection yet" moment, hearing a skeptic, an expert, and a big-picture voice argue it out is genuinely how many people think. Podcast-as-thinking-tool, grounded in your actual session.
Why parking it is rightIt multiplies model cost per round, it needs real audio direction (turn-taking, no crosstalk, interruptibility), it needs a strict safety boundary (panelists are narration-only; exactly one designated line back to the working agent, through the existing explicit send pipeline), and it is a new interaction paradigm to teach. Shipping it before responsiveness, privacy, and the harness wave would dilute all three.

The shape I would prototype later

  • Entry: drag a second personality onto the character during a focused session, or "Add to call…" from the ⇧⌘P palette. Your drag instinct matches the existing physicality.
  • Format: two or three personalities, role archetypes assignable (Skeptic, Subject Expert, Big Picture, User Advocate), bounded rounds (say three) then a synthesis turn, all compiled through the existing context compiler with a cost preview up front, same as whole-session review.
  • Exit: the roundtable ends with a spoken synthesis and one voicemail card capturing positions and open questions, so the listening session converts into an artifact you take back to the working agent. You then express your view through the normal Tell Agent path.
  • Steelman mode: your instinct not to frame it as debate is right. Prompt panelists to build the strongest version of each position, not to win.
